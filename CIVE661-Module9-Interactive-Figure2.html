How Many Modes Are Enough? — Modal Contribution Factors and Truncation Error

Under a static force distribution S, the response of each floor is built from contributions of all N modes. Each mode’s share is its modal contribution factor r̄n (the factors sum to 1). Choose how many modes J to include: the bars below highlight the modes kept, and the truncation error eJ = 1 − Σn≤J r̄n shows what is left out. Watch the approximate deflected shape converge to the exact one as J grows.
